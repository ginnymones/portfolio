## Problem Statement

ShopEase was losing 72% of users at checkout — significantly above the industry average of 55%. Analytics revealed that users were dropping off primarily at the shipping information step and when presented with payment options. Exit surveys pointed to confusion, distrust, and fatigue as primary reasons.

## Solution

I approached this as a research-first project:

1. **Competitive audit** — Analyzed checkout flows from 15 top e-commerce sites to identify patterns and opportunities
2. **Usability testing** — Ran moderated sessions with 12 participants to pinpoint exact friction points
3. **A/B testing** — Tested 4 design variations with live traffic before committing to a direction

### Design Changes

- Consolidated the 5-step checkout into a single-page flow with collapsible sections
- Added trust signals (security badges, return policy) at key decision points
- Introduced address auto-complete to reduce form fatigue
- Simplified payment to show saved methods first with one-tap checkout

## Results & Impact

- **35% reduction** in cart abandonment
- **$2.3M additional revenue** in the first quarter post-launch
- **12% increase** in repeat purchases due to saved preferences
- Average checkout time reduced from 4.5 minutes to 1.8 minutes
