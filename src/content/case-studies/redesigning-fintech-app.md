## Problem Statement

FinFlow's existing mobile app had a 60% drop-off rate during onboarding. Users found the account setup process confusing, with too many steps and unclear progress indicators. The app's navigation buried key features, leading to low engagement with core functionality like budgeting tools and transaction categorization.

## Solution

I led a full redesign focused on three pillars:

1. **Streamlined onboarding** — Reduced setup from 12 steps to 5, with clear progress indicators and the ability to skip non-essential steps
2. **Reorganized information architecture** — Conducted card sorting sessions with 20 users to restructure navigation around user mental models
3. **Progressive disclosure** — Introduced features gradually rather than overwhelming new users with everything at once

![Wireframes showing the before and after onboarding flow](/images/case-studies/fintech-wireframes.jpg)

### Key Design Decisions

- Used a bottom navigation bar with 4 primary actions (reduced from 6)
- Introduced a customizable dashboard so users see what matters most to them
- Added contextual tooltips that appear once and teach key interactions

![Final UI screens showing the redesigned app](/images/case-studies/fintech-screens.jpg)

## Results & Impact

- **40% increase** in user engagement within 30 days of launch
- **Drop-off rate reduced** from 60% to 22% during onboarding
- **App Store rating** improved from 3.2 to 4.6 stars
- **Time to first transaction** reduced by 65%

The redesign shipped to 50,000+ users and became the foundation for the company's design system.
